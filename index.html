A Bestie Letter 💌

One last message is inside.

Dear Mansi,

When I started making this website, I thought it would be a simple birthday wish.

But somewhere between searching old school photos, remembering our first call after 9 years, and thinking about how naturally you became important again, I realised something.

This is not really just about a birthday. It is about appreciating someone who became a very meaningful part of my life again after being gone from it for so long.

You have been there for me in moments where I did not even know how to explain what I was feeling. Sometimes life feels heavy, sometimes confusing, and sometimes I just need someone I can talk to without filtering my thoughts.

You are one of those very few people.

I know you often tell me not to put too much effort into things for you. But I think this is where we see things differently.

People who genuinely care, support, and stay in your life deserve to be respected and appreciated.

So please do not think of this as something excessive. Think of it as a small reminder that you are valued.

And honestly, making this was fun for me. I am from IT, so designing it, fixing it, adding memories, making the little lock, music, effects, and everything else felt like practice with emotions inside it.

My wish for you this year is simple: may you always have reasons to smile, may your travels take you to beautiful places, may your desserts always taste amazing, may your Mysore masala dosa always be perfect, and may life be kind to you in all the ways you deserve.

Strange how one person can become unintentionally important without either of you noticing when it happened.

Happy Birthday, Mansi.

Thank you for being one of the best parts of my second chapter.

— Ayush
